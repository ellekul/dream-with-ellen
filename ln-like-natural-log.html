About Me

Learning Enthusiast | Community Builder | Life Appreciator

My Professional Journey

My love of creative problem solving began as a student reading Art of Problem Solving textbooks and competing in math contests. In middle school, I founded a math club at my school and launched one at a nearby under-resourced school to share the excitement of problem-solving.

I went on to major in Mathematics at UC Berkeley as a Regents’ and Chancellor’s Scholar, where I served as President of Women in Mathematics and worked closely with the Berkeley and Stanford math circles. During those years, I designed curriculum and presented advanced topics, such as combinatorics and linear algebra, in engaging ways to students from elementary through high school.

After graduation, I joined the company that sparked my passion for math, Art of Problem Solving (AoPS), where I:

  • Opened AoPS’s first three Bay Area campuses
  • Led the Virtual Campus, achieving 30% growth in just 6 months
  • Served as Director of Engagement and Growth, focusing on marketing, website design, CRO testing, and communications

Although I loved strategic work, I missed being close to students. In 2025, I returned to the classroom as Campus Director at AoPS Academy Irvine, where I now am able to work directly with students in the classroom again, while continuing to utilize business strategy to grow our center (in the last 6 months, we have grown 38%).

My Personal Journey

My top five personal values right now: peace, wisdom, authenticity, growth, and love.

Outside of career, in my time off, I’ve traveled to 40+ countries, continuously learning, broadening my perspective, and sharing ideas across cultures and communities.

I believe deeply in presence: this moment will never come back again. I practice being in the present moment through journaling, meditation, yoga, reiki, and improv comedy.

I only am who I am due to the incredible human beings in my closest orbit. They inspire me, mirror back my best self, and act as, what my friend Jazz calls, “mutual blindspot checkers.” They remind me to move toward “what feels warm” (shoutout Sus). I feel endlessly blessed to learn from, grow with, and be inspired by the people who make me who I am today.
